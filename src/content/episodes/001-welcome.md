Welcome. SpeakForWater is a daily podcast that turns peer-reviewed water research
into a 10-minute morning listen. Every weekday, an automated pipeline scans
arXiv, OpenAlex, and a curated list of journals for the most important new
open-access water paper, then generates a natural conversation between two AI
hosts who unpack the methodology and findings.

## Why we built this

There are two facts that don't fit together. First, water is the defining
crisis of our time — droughts, floods, contamination, and aquifer depletion
affect billions. Second, the science addressing all of this is published
every day, but most of it stays locked in journals that few read.

We don't think AI replaces the work of researchers — it makes more people
engage with that work. Every episode here links straight to the original paper.
The hope is that you'll listen, learn, and then go read the science yourself.

## What to expect

Every episode follows the same structure: a 10-minute conversation between two
hosts, full show notes with key findings, links to the original paper, and a
complete transcript. New episodes drop every weekday at 7am UTC.
